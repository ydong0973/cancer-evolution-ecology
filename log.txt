the promise of fasting as a chemotherapeutic primer. The idea is that chemotherapy often has strong side effects on normal cells as well as cancer cells. However, fasting causes normal cells to exit the cell cycle and undergo arrest, whereas cancer cells are less affected by the lack of resources and undergo autonomous proliferation. This then allows the chemotherapeutic agent to differentiate among normal and cancer cells more effectively, improving its efficacy. Notably, this is not an effect that fasting alone can achieve: if used as the sole therapeutic modality, cancer cells would take over the population. There are other extensions that could be made to this base idea: baseline intermittent fasting, impact of fasting on the immune system, and perhaps even some of the cancer biosemiotics ideas I’ve been playing with. The base mathematical model would be competitive Lotka-Volterra consumer-resource, with perhaps an equation for immune attack as well.
